Is there a post-email Internet?
Using email to register in any service is so engrained that we do not even think about it.
But is there a way to use online services without having to provide your personal email?
Platforms could just generate a random account id and password and we would have to store this credentials. We would need a general credential manager such as last-pass.
Providing our emails can be problematic from a security point of view:
Our email can get hacked, if the email gets exposed, diferent attackers can track our accounts and can be used to try to break into our account with leaked or brute forced passwords.
We can also become a vector of attacks of phishing campaings
Almost every service provides the ability of hiding emails, GitHub, linkedin and even apple.
https://screenrant.com/apple-hide-my-email-address-privacy-feature-explained/ (Read to understadn why it's important)
If the credentials are generated by the applications, users can not use week passwords in the services which can be exploited.
So is there any way to have an internet that does not use emails for almost anything. We can simply have a centralised account management service app or a better identity service
This is also strongly related with oauth and SS0
Pose the question to reader, Is it possible to have an internet without emails? What would it belike? What other implications would it have?
